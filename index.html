Masters of the air.

Four species, four philosophies of flight. Each one perfected over millions of years of evolution.

Golden Eagle

A wingspan over two metres and eyesight eight times sharper than ours. The undisputed sovereign of mountain skies.

Hummingbird

Wings beating up to 80 times a second, hovering with impossible stillness. The only bird that can fly backwards.

Great Grey Owl

Silent flight and hearing so precise it can strike prey hidden beneath 30 centimetres of snow. A ghost of the northern forest.

Peregrine Falcon

The fastest animal alive. In a hunting dive it exceeds 380 km/h — faster than a Formula 1 car at full throttle.

Did You Know?

The Arctic Tern flies 70,000 km every year.

From pole to pole and back again — the longest migration on Earth. Over a lifetime, that's three round-trips to the Moon.
